# Business Impact Report — Gratuity Calculator

> Estimates below are directional planning figures based on the Indian search
> landscape for salaried-finance queries. Actual volumes/difficulty vary and shift
> seasonally; validate against Search Console + a keyword tool post-launch. Nothing
> here is a guarantee of ranking or traffic.

## Snapshot

| Metric                            | Estimate                                                                                                                                               |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Primary keyword**               | gratuity calculator                                                                                                                                    |
| **Monthly search volume (India)** | ~90,000–160,000 for the head term; ~300,000+ across the full cluster (gratuity calculation, formula, eligibility, tax, covered/not-covered long-tails) |
| **Keyword difficulty**            | Medium–High (~40–55) for the head term; Low–Medium (~15–35) for formula/how-to and long-tail                                                           |
| **Search intent**                 | Predominantly transactional/tool + informational (eligibility, tax, cap)                                                                               |
| **Expected CTR**                  | ~6–12% at positions 3–6; ~20–30% at positions 1–2 with an FAQ rich result                                                                              |
| **Seasonality**                   | Mild peaks around job-change/appraisal season (Mar–Jun) and at retirement; steady year-round                                                           |

## Top competitors

- **Groww** — high authority, ranks broadly for salary/retirement calculators.
- **ClearTax / ET Money** — strong brand and content depth.
- **BankBazaar / Paisabazaar** — broad calculator coverage, ad-heavy.
- **HR/payroll blogs (Razorpay, Zoho, greytHR)** — content on gratuity rules.
- **Government/Labour resources** — authoritative but poor UX.

**Where we win:** faster, private (in-browser), no signup/ads; covered vs
not-covered toggle (÷26 vs ÷30); explicit 6-month rounding rule; eligibility flag;
₹20 lakh cap visualised on the chart; taxability explained; CSV/share; visible
official sources (Payment of Gratuity Act 1972, Ministry of Labour, Section 10(10)).

## Expected internal links

- **Inbound to the calculator:** EPF + Income Tax + HRA (relatedTools already form
  the salary/retirement hub), homepage (featured), /popular, /new, sitemap, and all
  three supporting articles (primary CTA in each) → 6+ internal entry points at launch.
- **Outbound from the calculator:** epf, income-tax, hra, ppf, sip, fd (relatedTools)
  — routing users deeper into the salary → retirement ecosystem.
- **Article cross-links:** 3 articles interlink and each links the calculator + EPF +
  PPF + SIP + Income Tax (+ HRA where relevant).

## Estimated organic opportunity

- **Cluster ceiling:** given head-term volume and long-tail breadth, the gratuity
  topic can realistically drive **tens of thousands of organic sessions/month** at
  maturity, steady through the year with mild appraisal/retirement peaks.
- **High tool engagement:** strong transactional intent means a large share of clicks
  will use the calculator, lifting dwell time and internal navigation to EPF, Income
  Tax, HRA, PPF, and SIP — compounding authority across the whole hub.
- **Cluster compounding:** cross-linking with the shipped EPF, Income Tax, and HRA
  calculators strengthens the salary/retirement hub, lifting sibling pages.

## Why this calculator is strategically important

1. **Completes the core salary-exit trio.** Income Tax + HRA + EPF covered earning
   and saving; gratuity covers the **exit/retirement payout**, rounding out the
   salaried lifecycle and deepening topical authority.
2. **Bridges salary → retirement.** Gratuity naturally connects tenure and salary to
   retirement outcomes, reinforcing links to EPF/PPF/SIP and seeding future NPS and
   retirement-planning tooling.
3. **High, steady demand.** Gratuity is a perennial salaried query — searched at every
   job change and retirement — delivering durable, low-seasonality traffic.
4. **Low marginal cost.** Built entirely on the frozen Tool Framework with no new
   shared components or engine changes — pure additive SEO leverage.
5. **Defensible differentiation.** The covered/not-covered toggle, rounding rule,
   eligibility flag, cap visualisation, and taxability explainer address the exact
   confusions competitors gloss over.
