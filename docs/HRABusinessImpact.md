# Business Impact Report — HRA Calculator

> Estimates below are directional planning figures based on the Indian search
> landscape for salaried-taxpayer queries. Actual volumes/difficulty vary by tool
> and season; validate against Search Console + a keyword tool post-launch. Nothing
> here is a guarantee of ranking or traffic.

## Snapshot

| Metric                            | Estimate                                                                                                                                                           |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Primary keyword**               | hra calculator                                                                                                                                                     |
| **Monthly search volume (India)** | ~90,000–150,000 for the head term; ~300,000+ across the full cluster (incl. "hra exemption calculator", "house rent allowance calculator", city + rent long-tails) |

## Top competitors

- **ClearTax** — high authority, ranks for most tax calculators; signup-gated flows.
- **Groww / ET Money** — strong brand, app-first; content around calculators.
- **BankBazaar / Paisabazaar** — broad calculator coverage, ad-heavy.
- **Tax2win / Scripbox** — content depth on HRA rules.
- **Income Tax Department utility** — authoritative but poor UX.

**Where we win:** faster, private (in-browser), no signup/ads, shows all three
Rule 2A amounts with the winner highlighted and _why_, metro/non-metro toggle,
monthly + annual output, CSV/share, and visible official sources (E-E-A-T).

## Expected internal links

- **Inbound to the calculator:** Income Tax Calculator (relatedTools), homepage
  (featured), /popular, /new, sitemap, and all three supporting articles (primary
  CTA in each) → 6+ internal entry points at launch.
- **Outbound from the calculator:** income-tax, home-loan, ppf, sip, emi, fd
  (relatedTools) — routing users deeper into the salary/retirement ecosystem.
- **Article cross-links:** 3 articles interlink and each links the calculator +
  Income Tax + Home Loan + PPF/SIP.

## Expected supporting articles

Shipped in `content/articles/` (production-ready, not outlines):

1. **What is HRA?** — captures definition/concept queries; funnels to the calculator.
2. **How HRA Exemption is Calculated** — targets the how-to snippet; worked examples.
3. **HRA vs Home Loan Tax Benefits** — captures the high-value rent-vs-buy decision
   cluster and bridges to the Home Loan and Income Tax calculators.

Future expansion candidates (not in scope this sprint): "HRA in the New vs Old
Regime", "Section 80GG for those without HRA", "HRA for self-employed".

## Estimated organic opportunity

- **Cluster ceiling:** the HRA topic can realistically drive **tens of thousands of
  organic sessions/month** at maturity given the head-term volume and long-tail
  breadth, concentrated around the two seasonal peaks.
- **Conversion to tool use:** tool intent is high; a strong share of clicks will
  interact with the calculator, boosting dwell time and internal navigation to
  Income Tax, Home Loan, PPF, and SIP — compounding authority across the cluster.
- **Compounding effect:** by cross-linking with the already-shipped Income Tax
  Calculator, HRA strengthens the whole tax hub's topical authority, lifting sibling
  pages rather than competing with them.

## Why this calculator is strategically important

1. **Deepens the tax topical cluster.** Income Tax was the hub; HRA is the first
   high-volume spoke, signalling to search engines that Esytol comprehensively
   covers salaried taxation — the exact "topical authority" play in the brief.
2. **Bridges to the salary → EPF → gratuity → NPS → retirement journey.** HRA is the
   natural entry point into salary-component tooling, seeding future calculators
   (EPF, gratuity, NPS) with an interlinked, high-traffic anchor already in place.
3. **High commercial intent, low marginal cost.** Built entirely on the frozen Tool
   Framework with no new components or engine changes — pure additive SEO leverage.
4. **Seasonal, recurring demand.** Unlike one-off queries, HRA is searched every
   declaration and filing season, delivering durable, repeating traffic.
5. **Strong differentiation.** The three-rule transparency, metro toggle, and
   New-Regime caveat address the exact confusions competitors gloss over — a
   defensible UX and content edge.
