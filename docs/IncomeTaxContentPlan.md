## Article 1 — "Income Tax Slabs for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27): New vs Old Regime"

**Primary keyword:** income tax slabs 2025-26 · **Intent:** informational
**Meta description:** The complete income tax slab tables for FY 2025-26 under
both the New and Old regimes, with the ₹12 lakh rebate and cess explained.

**Outline**

1. Intro — what a "slab" is and why FY 2025-26 changed (Finance Act 2025).
2. New Regime slab table (0 / 5 / 10 / 15 / 20 / 25 / 30%) + ₹75k standard deduction.
3. Old Regime slab table (0 / 5 / 20 / 30%) + ₹50k standard deduction + deductions.
4. Section 87A rebate — nil tax up to ₹12L (New) / ₹5L (Old); marginal relief at ₹13L.
5. Surcharge tiers + 4% Health & Education Cess.
6. Worked example: ₹15L salary under each regime.
7. **CTA:** "Skip the maths — [calculate your exact tax](/tools/income-tax-calculator)."

**Internal links:** calculator (primary CTA + inline); PPF & SIP calculators where
80C investments are mentioned; home-loan calculator at the §24(b) interest note.

## Article 2 — "Old vs New Tax Regime: Which Should You Choose in 2025-26?"

FY 2025-26 — break-even deductions, who each suits, and how to decide.

**Outline**

1. The core trade-off: lower New-regime rates vs Old-regime deductions.
2. What you give up in the New Regime (80C, 80D, HRA, §24b, etc.).
3. Break-even deduction analysis — the deduction level where Old beats New at
   ₹10L / ₹15L / ₹20L income.
4. Profiles: high-rent salaried, home-loan borrower, no-deductions earner.
5. Why the New Regime is now the default (and how to opt for Old).

## Article 3 — "How Income Tax Is Calculated in India (Step by Step)"

**Primary keyword:** how income tax is calculated · **Intent:** informational
**Meta description:** A step-by-step walkthrough of how income tax is computed in
India for FY 2025-26 — gross income, deductions, slab tax, rebate, surcharge, cess.

**Outline**

1. Step 1 — total your income (salary + other income).
2. Step 2 — subtract the standard deduction and eligible deductions → taxable income.
3. Step 3 — apply the slab rates to taxable income.
4. Step 4 — apply the §87A rebate (and marginal relief just above the threshold).
5. Step 5 — add surcharge for high incomes (with marginal relief).
6. Step 6 — add 4% Health & Education Cess → total tax; then effective & monthly tax.
7. Fully worked example following one salary through all six steps.
8. **CTA:** "Run these six steps automatically →
   [Income Tax Calculator](/tools/income-tax-calculator)."

**Internal links:** calculator (primary); EMI/home-loan calculators (loan interest
in deductions); GST calculator (as another India tax tool, in a "related" strip);
cross-links to Articles 1 and 2.
